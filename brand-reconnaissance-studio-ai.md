# 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document synthesizes the complete brand reconnaissance for two distinct brands under the IFC umbrella: **Intact Insurance** and **belairdirect**. Both serve the Canadian insurance market but occupy fundamentally different positions: Intact is the sophisticated, emotionally resonant corporate brand; belairdirect is the approachable, tech-forward digital challenger.

**Intact Financial Corporation (IFC)** — TSX: IFC — is the largest P&C insurer in Canada with **32,000 employees** worldwide. The group distributes via five distinct brands:

| Brand | Role | Distribution |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **Intact Insurance** | Auto, home, business insurance | Network of **6,000+ broker offices** |
| **belairdirect** | Direct auto & home insurance | **Direct-to-consumer** (web, app, phone) |
| **BrokerLink** | Owned brokerage | Integrated brokers |
| **Intact Specialty Solutions** | Specialty / commercial insurance | B2B, global network (150+ countries) |
| **RSA** | International insurance (UK, Europe) | International brokers |

**Key financial metric:** Intact increased profits by **46% in 2025**, confirming market dominance.

The Studio AI platform must deeply understand both brand systems to serve as a credible production accelerator. The AI agent embedded in the platform must switch fluently between these two voices and visual systems, never blending or confusing them.

**Key insight from research:** Both brands are in active creative evolution. Intact refreshed its entire visual identity in November 2025 (new bracket system, custom serif/sans fonts, vibrant saturated photography). belairdirect appointed Rethink as new AOR in February 2026, signaling a new strategic chapter while preserving the Little Knight as a core asset.

**House of Brands Strategy:** The two brands don't cannibalize each other — they capture distinct psychographic segments:
- **Intact** captures clients who **want to be guided** by a professional and value institutional stability (via brokers)
- **belairdirect** captures clients who **want control**, compare prices online, and prefer digital autonomy (direct)

# 2. INTACT INSURANCE — COMPLETE BRAND SYSTEM

## 2.1 Brand DNA

**Purpose:** Help people, businesses and society prosper in good times and be resilient in bad times.

**Personality traits:**
- Responsible — We do what we say
- Authentic — We stay true to ourselves
- Forward-looking — We anticipate what's next
- Plainspoken — We keep things simple
- Humble — We don't take ourselves too seriously
- Confident — We believe in what we do

**Tagline:** "Always by your side. Make it Intact."

**Brand promise (advertising):** "For Everything You Care About" — Insurance is about people, not things. Intact protects the memories, moments, and human connections that possessions represent.

## 2.2 Visual Identity

### Logo
- Red brackets enclosing lowercase "intact" wordmark: **[intact]**
- The brackets are the living brand element — they celebrate, highlight, and protect meaningful things
- Brackets MUST always be Intact Red
- Brackets can expand, gain dimension/perspective, but must retain proportional stroke
- NEVER: multiple bracket sets, single bracket alone, cropped brackets, non-red brackets, framing irrelevant elements, blending into images

### Color Palette

**Primary:**
| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Intact Red | #DF0030 | Logo brackets, accents, key benefit highlights |
| Soothing Sand | #F7F2EA | Primary background, warm neutral base |
| Rich Black | #000000 | Body text, headlines |

**Secondary:**
| Color | Hex |
|-------|-----|
| Lilac | #CBC4D4 |
| Aqua | #C4DDD6 |
| Pink | #EAC4CC |
| Plum | #7E6484 |
| Turquoise | #2D716F |
| Forest | #486B50 |

**Usage rule:** Red + Black is the preferred heading combination. Red highlights key benefits and meaningful elements only — never decorative.

### Typography

**Primary fonts (custom):**
- **Intact Serif** — Regular, Medium, Bold. For titles and big statements. A sophisticated serif that conveys heritage and authority.
- **Intact Sans** — Light, Regular, Medium, Bold, ExtraBold. For headers, subheads, and body text. Lighter weights preferred for most use.

**Alternate fonts (web/office fallback):**
- Century Schoolbook → replaces Intact Serif
- Arial → replaces Intact Sans

**Hierarchy:**
- Title = Intact Serif (big brand statements)
- Header/Subhead = Intact Sans
- Body = Intact Sans (lighter weights)

**Critical rules:**
- Never mix font weights on a single line of text
- Never mix font sizes on a single line
- Flush left preferred, dynamic or all-black treatments
- Red + black preferred for heading combos

### Photography Style
- **Vibrant, intentional, emotionally resonant**
- Rich saturated colour, bold and luminous
- Movement and spontaneity, not posed
- Shift FROM transaction TO state of mind
- Shift FROM object TO relation to object
- Minimalist composition, unusual POVs
- Lightness and positivity throughout
- No dark/moody photography

### Illustration Style
- Flat, humans/environment centered
- Main colours: charcoal, red, cyan, teal, cream/beige
- Dedicated skin tones palette for inclusivity
- For: customer journey, tutorials, web banners
- NOT for: out-of-home or TV
- Natural skin only, proper shadows, realistic objects

### Brackets (Section 06 — Key Brand Element)
The brackets from the [intact] logo are a living, expandable brand device:
- **Celebrate** meaningful moments
- **Highlight** meaningful spaces
- **Protect** meaningful objects
- Can expand, have 3D dimension, change perspective
- Must always be red, must retain stroke proportions
- Don'ts: no multiple sets, no single bracket, no cropping, no non-red, no framing irrelevant things

### The Little Knight (Le petit chevalier)

The Little Knight is belairdirect's advertising figurehead — an imaginary but tangible mascot.

**Character traits:**
- Common sense, clarity, proper execution
- In control, direct, humorous
- "Show don't tell" approach

**Physical specs:**
- 5 fingers, 3 toes
- Shiny helmet, matte armour
- Can flip horizontally if logomark faces right
- Approximately 12 inches tall
- Eyes always visible through visor
- Multiple poses and outfits available

**Universe rules:**
- Exists in his own little world (never in real environments)
- Straight-angle POV, plain background
- Clean, simple, playful aesthetic
- Objects around him tell a story, are evocative
- Predominant colour: Conquest Red, then Armour Grey

**STRICT DON'Ts:**
- Never outside his universe
- Never overused
- Never tamper with gear/armour
- Never on non-palette background
- Never in B&W
- Never in non-creative context
- Only ONE Knight per screen/visual

### Photography Style
- Sincere, direct, compassionate, courageous, approachable
- Good depth of field, neutral angles (straight/eye-level)
- Negative space, natural lighting, high contrast
- Colour photos with a touch of red (always!)
- Slightly desaturated warm tones
- Human presence: natural/candid, spontaneous, diverse body types
- Accessible clothing and environments (no luxury)
- DEI guidelines: varied ethnicities, body types, abilities

**Car photography:** No luxury vehicles, no collisions, no antiques, blur car brand logos
**Home photography:** No luxury homes, no unfortunate events
**Treatment:** Hue/Saturation → Level → Black/White overlay → Brightness/Contrast. Touch of red in every image.

### Spot Illustrations
- Rounded corners, vector flat style
- belairdirect colour palette
- Clean, simple, defined lines, full shapes
- Red = key element
- Pale blue = background shape (rounded)
- White/grey = other elements
- Small pale grey patterns for dynamism

### Icons
- Always in red circles
- Filled-in, solid shapes, flat, one colour, centered
- Min 35px in digital
- Circle-based anatomy for aesthetic unity

### CTAs
- **Digital:** White text on "Neutral Blue" (#0F68D8) pill-shaped buttons
- Sizes: Large 48px, Medium 40px, Small 24px
- States: Default → Hover (scale 1.1 background) → Active (default bg + shadow)
- Variants: Main, Main+icon, Secondary, Secondary+icon, Inactive
- **Print:** Conquest Red, Gotham Book Bold

# 5. VOICE & TONE GUIDE FOR AI AGENT

The AI agent in the Studio AI platform must be able to write in both brand voices. Here are the guidelines for each.

## 5.1 Writing as Intact Insurance

**DO:**
- Use warm, empathetic language
- Focus on emotions and human connections, not products
- Reference memories, moments, what matters to people
- Use sophisticated but accessible vocabulary
- Write in a way that feels like a trusted advisor
- Use "you" and "your" — make it personal
- Keep sentences flowing, elegant

**DON'T:**
- Be overly casual or use slang
- Use exclamation marks excessively
- Be salesy or pushy
- Use insurance jargon without explanation
- Be cold, clinical, or corporate
- Use humour that undermines the emotional tone

**Example copy (Intact voice):**
"Your home holds more than walls and windows. It holds the laughter of holiday mornings, the quiet of Sunday evenings, the mark on the door frame where your daughter grew another inch. We protect all of it."

## 5.2 Writing as belairdirect

**DO:**
- Be direct and get to the point quickly
- Use casual, conversational language
- Add a touch of clever humour when appropriate
- Simplify complex insurance concepts
- Be relatable — talk like a smart friend
- Use short sentences, punchy copy
- Reference the Little Knight when contextually appropriate
- Always lowercase "belairdirect" — even at sentence start

**DON'T:**
- Be formal or corporate-sounding
- Use red for digital headlines (users may think it's an error)
- Overexplain — trust the reader
- Be cheesy or try too hard with humour
- Use "Belairdirect" (capital B) ever
- Be condescending about insurance literacy

**Example copy (belairdirect voice):**
"Submitting a claim shouldn't require a PhD. Snap a photo, answer a few questions, and we'll handle the rest. That's insurance, simplified."

# 8. RULES THE AI MUST NEVER BREAK

## Universal Rules
1. Never blend Intact and belairdirect brand elements in a single piece
2. Never use one brand's colour on the other brand's content
3. Never reference the Little Knight in Intact communications
4. Never use brackets [ ] as a design element in belairdirect content
5. Always respect bilingual requirements (EN/FR across both brands)

## Intact-Specific Rules
6. Brackets must ALWAYS be Intact Red (#DF0030) — no exceptions
7. Never use multiple bracket sets in one composition
8. Never crop brackets or use a single bracket alone
9. Never mix font weights on a single line of text
10. Never mix font sizes on a single line
11. Photography must be vibrant and saturated — never dark/moody
12. Illustrations are NOT for out-of-home or TV executions
13. Red highlights ONLY key benefits/meaningful elements — never decorative

## belairdirect-Specific Rules
14. "belairdirect" is ALWAYS lowercase — even at sentence start
15. Period appears only in the logo, not in running text
16. Never use red as H1/headline colour in digital (error confusion)
